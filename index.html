Addiction information

The addict is the one who finds a convincing justification and incentive to repeat the addictive activity despite its harmful effects and serious consequences.

Feeling the need to take the drug or addictive substance daily. Gradual intake of larger quantities over time, due to the absence of the effect of the quantities that the body is accustomed to. Ensure that sufficient quantities of the addictive substance are available, and fear that it will be discontinued. Giving material priority to buying drugs or addictive drugs.

Dispensing anti-withdrawal drugs caused by quitting addiction. Dispensing medications that help focus or sleep, according to the patient's need and health condition. Admitting the patient to a specialized clinic. Organizing individual or family therapy sessions. Focus on understanding the nature of addiction, becoming drug-free and preventing relapse.
